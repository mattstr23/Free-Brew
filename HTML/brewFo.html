A Little History

Life and beer just seem to go together. From your standard Anheuser-Busch product, to a local micro brew down the street, it really does hit the mark on any kind of weather. Brewing and beers have been a part of human culture for ages. America started seeing the first breweries pop up in the 17th century under the Dutch West India Trading Company. From those beginnings and through prohibition, brewing has survived and in recent times, THRIVED! Whether you think of a large brewery like AB or Coors, to the over 8,000 micro breweries across the states, plenty of styles and unique looks are out there to enjoy!

Breweries

As mention before, breweries are everywhere across the states and even the world. Below are the main different types of breweries you'll find

    Macro Breweries

    The biggies of brewing (Anheuser-Busch, Miller, Heineken)

    Micro Breweries

    Micro breweries cover a very wide range. From the bigger guys just not big enough like Sam Adams and Leinenkugel, to your more localized bigger favorite brewery spots you might venture to.

    Nano Breweries

    These are your really small local brewery or even restaurant that creates they're own in house beer on a small scale
